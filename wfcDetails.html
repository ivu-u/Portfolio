Game Pitch

Prepare for an adventure through a sweets themed world, and help the sibling duo, Pan and Mochi, navigate a world of dangerous sweetures.

During campfire events, engage in heartwarming conversations and build bonds between characters as you gather new bonbon recipes. Lead your characters into intense battles where strategy is key and harness the power of 'bonbons' to customize and enhance your fighters' abilities. Combine these delectable treats to craft unique combinations that will turn the tide of battle in your favor.

Teamwork is the key to victory, and the strength of your bonds will determine your success. Unite with your main characters, plan out your combos, and discover how strong the bond of family truly is!

Event Sequencer

The EventSequencer system allows dialogue events to be triggered from the Battle State Machine, meaning dialogue can be triggered during turn based combat.

Each event has its own conditions that must be fulfilled for the event to start. For example, an event may be triggered if a certain character/enemy is inflicted by a specific status condition or if a character has low health.

The dialogue logic is implemented using Yarn Spinner and allows developers to easily implement dialogue scripts by reading text from a .txt file.


Enemy AI

Bonbon's combat is turn based, so one of the challenges of this project was to make turn-based AI that can make semi-intelligent decisions.

To do this, we decided on a weighted decision-making system. Every turn, the AI analyzes every action that it can make based on our custom weight values. For example, every one point of damage can be worth 5 points, inflicting the burn status effect is 40 points, and knocking out a character is worth 200 points. This system allows the AI to decide what action to take based on customizable priorities.

In the future, I am going to add a difficulty scaling option for the enemies, so they get stronger the longer the battle goes on (to scale with the buffs, the player is getting) and more complex decision-making behavior based on more factors like skill synergies with an enemy partner.
